6. Policy and Borders

China - Inbound Visa-Free Results
China's expanded visa-free policy (now covering 50+ economies including Australia, New Zealand, UK, Canada, France, Germany, South Korea, Singapore, and Malaysia) is delivering measurable results. Q1 2026: inbound foreign trips rose 30% year on year, with 8.32 million visa-free entries. Policy confirmed extended through December 31, 2026. Australian and New Zealand passport holders can enter China visa-free for 30 days. For your Accor China properties: this is a structural tailwind and a talking point for members who travel to China for business. (VizaHQ, Apr 23; VizaHQ, Apr 25)
Vietnam - 10-Year Golden Visa
Vietnam's Tourism Advisory Board is advancing a 10-year Golden Visa proposal toward mid-2026 implementation. Three categories: Golden Visa (5-10 years for long-stay tourists/residents), Investor Visa (10 years with permanent residency pathway), and Talent Visa (5 years). If enacted, this would create a long-stay, high-spend visitor profile in Vietnam that would improve ADR and hotel revenue at Accor's Vietnam portfolio. A membership pitch to long-stay visitors in Vietnam becomes easier when they are planning multi-year stays. (IMI Daily; reported Apr 27, 2026)
Thailand - Visa Exemption Review
Thailand's cabinet is reviewing the 60-day visa exemption following documented cases of abuse, with a potential revision to 30 days under discussion. If tightened, lower-value visitor volumes may fall while premium travelers (who can comply with stricter requirements) continue arriving. This is a positive for ADR and for the membership conversion story at premium hotels targeting the "high-value, long-stay" traveler Thailand is explicitly trying to attract. (MovingTo, 2026)
Indonesia - OTA Licensing Enforcement
Indonesia's March 31, 2026 deadline for unregistered accommodation providers to obtain valid operating licences has passed. The enforcement phase is now active: non-compliant properties face removal from OTA platforms. This reduces informal supply competition for branded hotel partners in Bali and Jakarta, a positive for occupancy and ADR at Accor Indonesia properties. (TTG Asia, Jan 2026)
Japan - Kyoto Hotel Tax (Active)
Kyoto's steeply tiered lodging tax (effective March 1, 2026) is embedded in market pricing. Rates above JPY 100,000/night (approx. AUD 1,000+) now attract JPY 10,000/person/night tax, up from JPY 1,000 previously. Revenue projected to nearly double to JPY 12.6 billion annually. Midscale and upscale Accor properties (the bulk of your Japan portfolio) are in the lower tax brackets and are unaffected. The tax creates a meaningful cost step-up at ultra-luxury properties where competitors operate. (Japan Travel, Kyoto tax; The Points Guy)
