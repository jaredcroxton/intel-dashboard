06 Policy and Borders

Thailand: visa-free stay likely cut from 60 days to 30 days. The Thai Ministry of Tourism and Ministry of Foreign Affairs have signaled that the 60-day visa-free experiment is ending. A Cabinet ratification proposal was moving through the Thai government process in late April 2026, with the aim of halving the visa-exempt period back to its original 30-day limit. No implementation date is confirmed yet, but the direction is clear. This will reduce the viability of Thailand as a long-stay base for digital nomads and slow-travel visitors, compressing future mid-week occupancy at economy and mid-scale properties. (Khaosod English, 25 Apr 2026)
Thailand: 300-baht air arrival fee pending Cabinet approval. Thailand's Deputy PM has tabled a 300-baht (~USD 9) tourist fee for all foreign air arrivals, to be embedded in airfares similar to Japan's departure tax. The levy would fund tourism infrastructure and include automatic travel insurance for arriving visitors. No Cabinet approval date has been set. Adds cost to inbound to Thailand, potentially nudging rate-sensitive travelers to Vietnam or Malaysia. (Wego Travel Blog, 2026; Thai Examiner, Apr 2026)
Japan: departure tax triples to JPY 3,000 from July 1, 2026. Japan's ruling coalition finalized the increase in the Tax Reform Outline for FY2026. The tax rises from JPY 1,000 to JPY 3,000 per departing person (air and sea). Funds go to overtourism infrastructure. Practically invisible to travelers (embedded in airfares) but adds approximately USD 14 per person to the total cost of visiting Japan. Tax-free shopping reform effective November 1, 2026, will shift tourists to a consumption-tax-on model, reducing the duty-free shopping incentive. (Travel Voice, 2026)
Philippines: ASEAN Summit in Cebu, May 6-8. The 48th ASEAN Summit and Related Meetings convene in Cebu City this week, generating short-term MICE demand in a market that has otherwise underperformed on leisure arrivals in April. A useful prompt for hotel partners in Cebu on why membership drives consistent revenue vs. sporadic MICE peaks. (Travel news, May 2026)
Malaysia: Digital Arrival Card (MDAC) required pre-arrival. All visitors to Malaysia must complete the MDAC online within three days before arrival. No new changes in the window, but friction at border remains relevant context for high-frequency travelers comparing destinations. (MakeYourAsia, 2026)
Singapore full-year arrivals target: 17-18 million. STB published its 2026 tourism receipts forecast at SGD 31.0-32.5 billion. No new visa changes in the window. Steady and predictable, which supports Accor Plus hotel partners in the city confident in baseline demand. (Travel and Tour World, May 2026)
