Section 6

Policy & Borders

South Korea extends Indian group visa fee waiver to June 2027. Announced May 1, 2026. The Ministry of Justice extended the C-3-2 short-term group visa fee waiver (18,000 won per person) for authorized Indian tour groups of five or more through June 30, 2027. Previously set to expire June 2026. Applications processed by authorized agencies within five working days. This sustains the incentive that drove Indian arrivals to Korea up 37% in 2025 and is relevant to MICE, incentive and leisure group segments. (VisaHQ, May 1 2026)
Thailand 300-baht tourist entry fee: still pending, no confirmed launch date. Thailand's incoming government fast-tracked a proposal for a 300-baht (approximately USD 9) air-arrival fee for all foreign visitors. The fee is split: 70 baht toward mandatory visitor insurance, 230 baht toward tourism infrastructure. As of May 5, the proposal is still awaiting formal Cabinet approval and no collection mechanism or start date has been announced. The Nation Thailand reported in April the Tourism Ministry was targeting a mid-2026 rollout, but that timeline is not confirmed. Monitor for Cabinet announcements. (Nation Thailand, Apr 2026)
Southeast Asia aviation connectivity under pressure. Fuel cost increases tied to the US-Iran conflict have led Chinese carriers to suspend or reduce flights on several Southeast Asia routes (Xi'an-Phuket, Xiamen-Vientiane among confirmed cuts). This is not a border policy change but affects effective access to some Thai and Lao markets. No government responses announced within the window. Monitor for TAT or Thai government statements on route-restoration support. (TTG Asia, May 1 2026)
