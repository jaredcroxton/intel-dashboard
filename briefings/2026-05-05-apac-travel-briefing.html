06 / Policy and Borders

Visa changes, levies, OTA rules, and border updates

JAPAN: Departure tax triples from JPY 1,000 to JPY 3,000 from July 1, 2026
Confirmed in the FY2026 Tax Reform Outline by the ruling coalition. Applies to all travelers over two years old departing by air or sea. Revenue target: JPY 130 billion annually, earmarked for sustainable tourism infrastructure. Simultaneously, at least 20 municipalities have introduced local accommodation levies, with Kyoto charging JPY 10,000 per night at luxury properties. For your members: these add-on costs favour longer stays at known properties with guaranteed member rates over unpredictable short-stay itineraries. Sources: Nation Thailand, 2026 / Travel Voice Japan, 2026
CHINA: 45-country visa-free policy extended through December 31, 2026
China confirmed the extension of its unilateral 30-day visa-free policy for nationals of 45 countries and territories through end-2026. Canadian and UK nationals can visit China for up to 30 days without a visa. Chinese consulates in Australia, NZ, Canada, Singapore, UK, and Ireland are also waiving fingerprint collection for short-term visa applicants through end-2026. This sustains outbound travel demand from China and makes APAC hotel stays accessible to Western travelers connecting via China. Source: Clark Hill, Feb 2026
PHILIPPINES: Chinese nationals now visa-free for 14 days (effective January 16, 2026)
China nationals can enter the Philippines visa-free for up to 14 days for tourism or business. This is a material demand signal for Philippine hotel markets, particularly Manila, Boracay, and Palawan. Properties in those markets will see stronger occupancy from the Chinese leisure segment through 2026. Source: Clark Hill, Feb 2026
OTA DISTRIBUTION: "OTA 2.0" debate surfaces at Skift Asia Forum
A Skift Asia Forum panel on April 29 (title: "Investors Bet on OTA 2.0 - But Some Builders Aren't There Yet") surfaced a growing divide between hotel owners who want direct bookings and OTAs investing in AI-powered search and conversion. The shift toward AI-driven travel planning may reduce organic OTA discovery and make loyalty programme membership, which drives direct booking, more defensible as a long-term value proposition for hotel owners. This is a structural argument for why Accor Plus benefits hotel partners as well as members. Source: Skift, 29 Apr 2026
